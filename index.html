The Maple Street Mansion

The Maple Street Mansion, a vibrant landmark in Carrollton, Georgia, has captivated residents and visitors alike since its construction in 1890. Built by L.C. Mandeville, a prominent figure in the local textile industry, the mansion holds a unique place in the city’s heart.

In the 1960s, it was divided into boutiques and housing, even earning the spirited nickname “Hippie Joint.” In 1984, the Uglum family purchased the property, ushering in a long period of growth.

Now, the Mansion embraces a new spring chapter as a showcase for local creators. Saved from demolition in 2014, it continues to thrive as a testament to love, family, and our local community.

Our Legacy

Our journey began in 2013, rooted in healing and the power of creation.

After loss, I found a path forward through artistry—taking forgotten items and giving them new life. Today, that spirit of resurrection is woven into every corner of the Heirloom Store.

We believe life is about the legacy we leave behind. Each piece we curate speaks of resilience, beauty, and the connections that bind us together across generations.
